GOOD NEWS CLUB

 

Starting off as a small church-based initiative, we have seen our group grow from 5 to 25 children in just a few months. Our organization partnered with the Burlington Care Centre in establishing this programme with children between the ages of 6 to 11 years old. We now meet weekly on a Saturday.

The main focus of this club is to focus on moral regeneration targeting a younger generation. In running this group, we are becoming more aware of the many aspects of the Burlington community which destroys positive and constructive character building in children. We also see this club as a means of reducing children’s attraction to life and soul-destroying distractions on the streets.

Amongst the areas we focus on are:


-What makes people good people or bad people.
-What are the rights and responsibilities of children.
-How do we as children show respect for elders and other community members.
-When we or other people are hurt, how can we deal with our or their hurt and anger.
-Our actions have consequences. When we do right what happens and when we do wrong what happens.

 

Besides helping children do a values-based reflection on and be practical about various challenges they face, this club provides the children with some escape from the volatile environments they live in. As they can be very open at times, we are careful to provide a safe and confidential environment in which to communicate. Sometimes we have to take individual children aside to address very personal matters with them.
Nicky the club facilitator notes: “The children were especially excited when we provided them with some stationary, needed for the activities of the club . . .I have seen an incredible change in attitude and level of respect since they've joined Good News Club, (they) want to be better at what they do every week.
The children are very enthusiastic and show a real need for the club’s activities.
Nicky, Facilitator of the Good News Club helping out at the year-end Toy Story
